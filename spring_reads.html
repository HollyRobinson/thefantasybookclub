Spring Reads 2024


Spring is a season of renewal. It's also the perfect time to dive into a fresh reading list that captures the essence of the season. Whether you're looking for stories filled with magical transformations, blossoming romances, or tales that reflect the vibrant energy of spring, we've curated a list of must-read books for 2024. So, get ready to immerse yourself in these spring reads!

1. Splintered by A.G. Howard

In her 16th year, Alyssa Gardner is sucked into the not-so-mythical Wonderland. Here she must pass many tests to save her family from a curse. But she is faced with challenges along the way, like not knowing who she can trust. This book is set in the twisted world of Underland, where all manner of creatures reside, and the main character can hear the bugs and flowers talk. When I think of spring, I think of Wonderland. And this dark retelling of the well-known classic by Lewis Carroll will undoubtedly make you yearn for spring.

2. Wicked Lovely by Melissa Marr

Aislinn is a human gifted with the sight and is drawn into the world of faeries. Well, one faerie's world in particular. That of Keenan, the Summer King. When the Winter Queen killed the former king, a curse was placed on Keenan's court. Summer will perish and the human world with it if Keenan does not find a Summer Queen. The rich imagery and setting of this book transport you to another world, one that is equally magical and evocative of springtime, even though it is not about the spring court.

3. Howl's Moving Castle by Diana Wynne Jones

Aislinn is a human gifted with the sight and is drawn into the world of faeries. Well, one faerie's world in particular. That of Keenan, the Summer King. When the Winter Queen killed the former king, a curse was placed on Keenan's court. Summer will perish and the human world with it if Keenan does not find a Summer Queen. The rich imagery and setting of this book transport you to another world, one that is equally magical and evocative of springtime, even though it is not about the spring court.

4. Daughter of the Moon Goddess bye Sue Lynn Tan

Xingyin, daughter of the Moon Goddess Chang'e, has lived in peace her whole life. However, the Celestial Kingdom had plans to ruin that peace. After they sensed her magic, Xignyin was forced to flee from the only home she had ever known. Through a series of challenges, she became Liwei's companion. The last place she thought she would end up is by the side of the crown prince of the kingdom, who forced her away from her mother. But by following this path, she has the chance to free her mother from her prison and possibly find love. The lush descriptions of nature and blossoming relationships mirror the season of renewal, making it a perfect read for those looking for a story of hope and resilience.

5. Flowerheart by Catherine Bakewell

Clara accidentally grows poisonous flowers in her father's chest. To heal him, she needs to perform a difficult spell, and the only person willing to help her is her old friend Xavier. However, she uncovers a dark plot happening in the Queendom, which she is the only one who can stop. This book is filled with beautiful, springtime floral imagery that will get you prepared for spring.

6. Six Crimson Cranes by Elizabeth Lim

The princess of Kiata Shiori'anma possesses magic, a rare gift in her kingdom. On the morning of her betrothal, she causes an accident that forestalls the wedding, but she catches the attention of her wicked stepmother, Raikama. Raikama banishes the princess and turns her brothers into cranes. If she speaks a word, her brothers will die. This story of transformation highlights the best aspects of the season like renewal, growth, and the power of love.

7. A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas

When nineteen-year-old Feyre kills a wolf in the woods, she's thrust into a perilous, magical realm by a fearsome creature demanding retribution. Taken to a land she only knew from legends, Feyre learns that her captor, Tamlin, is not always the beast he appears to be but one of the powerful, immortal faeries who once dominated her world. As she adjusts to her new life, her icy resentment towards Tamlin melts into a fiery passion. However, an ancient, malevolent force is rising in the faerie lands, and Feyre must find a way to stop it or risk losing Tamlin and his world forever. With its captivating blend of romance, adventure, and faerie lore, this book is the perfect read for spring, offering a thrilling escape into a world of magic and danger.

8. The Spellshop by Sarah Beth Durst

Kiela, a reclusive librarian at the Great Library of Alyssium, has always struggled with social interactions. Her life is upended when a revolution forces her and her sentient spider plant assistant, Caz, to flee with a collection of precious spellbooks. Seeking refuge on her childhood island, she encounters an overly helpful and attractive neighbor who won't leave her alone. In need of income, Kiela uses an old family recipe and a bit of illegal magic to grow berries and opens the island's first secret spellshop. "The Spellshop" is a heartwarming tale filled with mythical creatures, magic, and the cozy charm of a Hallmark rom-com, making it a perfect spring read.




I hope this list has introduced you to some exciting new reads to enjoy this spring. Happy reading, and may your spring be filled with the magic of a great book!
